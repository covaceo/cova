# Cova Counsel and Owner Review Checklist

**Internal document. Not public policy text.**
**Prepared:** July 21, 2026

The public Privacy Policy and Terms intentionally do not invent the following facts. Confirm them with the owner and qualified counsel before paid scale, formal provider contracts, or jurisdiction-specific enforcement language.

## Legal identity

- Contracting legal name or confirmed sole-proprietor formulation.
- State/country of establishment.
- Public business address that is safe and appropriate to publish.
- Registered-agent or service-of-process details if applicable.
- Ownership of the Cova and covadesk.com marks and software IP.

## Governing law and disputes

- Chosen governing law and exclusive/nonexclusive venue.
- Whether arbitration and a class-action waiver are appropriate and enforceable for target users.
- Required pre-dispute notices and consumer carve-outs.
- Countries or regions where Cova will not be offered.

Current Terms use jurisdiction-neutral court language and preserve mandatory consumer rights rather than guessing.

## Billing and consumer rights

- Confirm whether Pro is a subscription, one-time purchase, or not yet sold.
- Exact price, currency, billing interval, taxes, renewal timing, trial terms, and founding-price treatment.
- Operational cancellation path and effective date.
- Refund policy, exceptions, chargeback handling, failed payment, downgrade, and reactivation.
- Cooling-off, auto-renewal, and mandatory refund rights in target jurisdictions.
- Checkout clickwrap and material-term presentation before any charge.

Current Terms make billing conditional on an enabled external checkout and preserve non-waivable rights.

## Privacy program

- Verify the factual vendor/subprocessor list and execute required DPAs.
- Verify Vercel, Supabase, Google Workspace, Stripe, Discord, and provider data regions and transfer mechanisms.
- Set and operationally enforce retention periods for request logs, auth/security records, support messages, deleted-user backups, and incident evidence.
- Determine whether trade history, inferred risk behavior, or provider-account data receives special treatment in target jurisdictions.
- Confirm California, Colorado, Virginia, Connecticut, Utah, Texas, Oregon, Montana, EU/UK, Canada, and other applicable thresholds before adding jurisdiction-specific promises.
- Document privacy-request identity verification, authorized-agent handling, appeal rights, and response timelines.
- Confirm whether a cookie consent mechanism becomes necessary if analytics or nonessential tracking is introduced.

## Trading and regulatory posture

- Confirm that all public and in-product outputs remain retrospective education/journaling rather than individualized trading recommendations.
- Review CTA/commodity-trading-adviser, investment-adviser, introducing-broker, and marketing rules for intended markets.
- Confirm community moderation rules prohibit signals, copy trading, account management, solicitation, and misleading performance claims.
- Review all future testimonials for provenance, permission, compensation disclosure, and representative-results context.

## Passport and performance claims

- Confirm Cova ranks and Passport images are never represented as audited performance, broker verification, identity verification, funding approval, or certification.
- Preserve visible demo provenance on sample exports.
- Keep downloaded PNG behavior truthful: no Cova-hosted privacy, revocation, or expiry unless those controls are actually built server-side.
- Establish an anti-forgery and material-alteration enforcement process.

## Provider agreements

- Obtain Tradovate application approval and minimum history scope.
- Confirm ProjectX/TopstepX terms permit API-key authentication, encrypted session-token storage, and retrospective account/trade-history processing.
- Confirm branding and trademark permissions.
- Document revocation APIs and provider-required incident notification.
- Confirm whether provider data may be used in Passport exports or only private account review.

## Security governance

- Establish named incident owner and escalation contacts.
- Define severity levels, response targets, credential-rotation procedure, notification decision process, and evidence retention.
- Schedule independent penetration testing before describing the product as audited.
- Decide whether cyber/privacy insurance is appropriate.
- Keep public security language limited to verified controls; do not claim SOC 2, PCI certification, provider approval, or penetration testing without evidence.

## Required final legal review

Qualified counsel should review the final public Privacy Policy, Terms, signup assent, checkout disclosure, community rules, provider contract language, and account deletion flow after the legal identity, target jurisdictions, and billing model are confirmed.
